Project Idea

Overview

Buddy Hub is an idea for an app using a concept similar to dating apps to connect individuals and groups to form possible new friendships. I think this project would be beneficial to young people in an increasingly fragmented digital society. It will use data and AI to predict the compatibility of possible new friendships based on your existing networks and preferences.

Motivation

Buddy Hub is an interesting approach to forming new friendships, in a way that has been heavily saturated by dating apps but not applied more generally to helping people form new connections in person. In an increasingly online world, social media and technology have made it easier to stay connected to the people you already know but can in reality isolate us from making new deeper friendships. I believe Buddy Hub could be useful to address the challenge and pressures younger generations are feeling growing up in such a technology-mediated social context. Studies from the headspace national youth mental health survey show that 54% of young Australians feel a sense of loneliness and that 43% percent felt isolated which has only been exasperated by the impact of covid, I believe Buddy Hub would help to address some of these problems.

Description

Buddy Hub will be a mobile app that uses machine learning algorithms paired with artificial intelligence and data systems to create personalised recommendations on friendship compatibility. It would do this using their stated preferences, data collected from other app usage, location data, and personality profiling. It would also have an optional feature where users could connect their social media accounts to give the app access to their existing networks. It could be used by all ages but would be specifically marketed towards young people, who are the demographic most affected by media isolation right now. Some scenarios it might be useful in, include moving cities or countries, starting in a new industry, school or university; or starting a new hobby and looking for friends with similar interests. A typical customer experience using Buddy Hub would include an initial intake of basic information in the sign-up process followed by a deeper analysis of the users' personality type, hobbies, interests, and beliefs. They would then be able to create a profile and state what they were looking for, for example, someone could be looking to join a group of friends, looking for a study buddy, or even just someone to talk to. They would then be shown profiles of people who are compatible with their profile and given access to message them through the app. Possible challenges or setbacks the app might face may be privacy issues, regarding the information shared by the users and who could have access to that information, such as personal data and identifying details. There would also be potential for identity theft, fraud, catfishing, or abusive behavior. To address these problems we would have to use clear and transparent protocols around identity transparency which may include a verification process to keep users traceable and accountable. Another possible obstacle would be that the app relies on a critical mass of users to operate effectively in finding compatible matches. A possible solution to this would be to focus the initial launch and marketing strategy in a confined geographic location which can then spread to other areas once gained popularity and customer confidence.

Tools and Technologies

The first stage would be to design and build a minimal viable product (MVP) using free open source tools such as Google App Engine and Google Recommendations AI. No specific hardware would be needed at this stage and data would be stored in Google Cloud during the development period. Google App Engine supports apps built in Python and other languages and is a fully managed environment. Google Recommendations AI allows you to build an end to end personalised recommendation system based on machine learning. Sample data could be used for initial testing and prototyping.

Skills Required

In order to write the application from scratch I would need advanced programming skills in a language such as Python, Ruby, C+ or Java. Another option while I’m learning to code is to use a drag and drop app builder such as Appy Pie or Good Barber that creates HTML-5 hybrid apps that work with all platforms. While I am designing and prototyping the app I could learn to use tools such as InVision or AdobbeXD to create an interactive screen prototype to test with the target demographic. I would need to develop additional skills and knowledge in how to set up and integrate a machine learning system using Google Recommendations AI. It would also be possible to outsource to a machine learning engineer to build the framework and algorithm for recommendations.

Outcome

If the project was successful a positive outcome would result in a high uptake of customers, who are satisfied with the service they have been provided by being connected with new friends they are compatible with. The result of this would be an increase in a sense of belonging and community for the younger generations and therefore a reduction in young people feeling lonely and isolated. The impact of this would be a more positive use of data and social media to bring people together in forming deeper and more meaningful relationships, rather than the current superficial casual connections.
